Appam

Description

Appam is a popular breakfast recipe in Kerala and Tamil Nadu. Made from fermented batter of rice, udad dal, and coconut, these spongy hoppers are typically eaten with curries, chutneys, or coconut milk.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup raw rice
  • 1/4 cup cooked rice
  • 1/4 cup grated coconut
  • 1/2 teaspoon active dry yeast or 1/4 cup fermented appam batter (to aid fermentation)
  • 1/2 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/2 cup coconut milk
  • Salt to taste
  • Water, as needed

Instructions

  1. Wash the raw rice thoroughly and soak it in water for about 4-6 hours or overnight.
  2. Drain the soaked rice and grind it along with cooked rice, grated coconut, and a little water to a smooth batter. The batter should be of pouring consistency. Not too thick nor too thin.
  3. Mix the yeast with sugar in a little warm water and let it sit for 5-10 minutes until it becomes frothy.
  4. Add the frothy yeast mixture to the batter and mix well.
  5. Allow the batter to ferment for about 6-8 hours or overnight. If you have previously fermented appam batter, you can use it as a starter to quicken the fermentation process.
  6. Add coconut milk to the batter. Adjust the consistency with water if needed.
  7. Add salt to taste and mix well.
  8. Heat an appam pan or a non-stick skillet over medium heat.
  9. Pour a ladle of batter into the center of the pan and quickly swirl the pan to spread the batter in a thin layer, leaving a slightly thicker center.
  10. Cover the pan with a lid and cook until the edges become crisp and golden, and the center is soft and spongy.
  11. Repeat the process with the remaining batter.
  12. Serve the appams hot with coconut milk, or any curry of your choice.
