Prawn Masala

Description

Prawn Masala is a delicious and flavorful Indian dish that features succulent prawns cooked in a rich and aromatic masala (spice) gravy.

Ingredients

    For the Marinade

  • 500g large prawns, cleaned and deveined
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice

    • For the Masala Paste

  • 1 cup grated coconut
  • 1 large onion, roughly chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste/li>
  • 1 tablespoon fennel seeds
  • 1 tablespoon poppy seeds
  • 1 teaspoon fennel seeds
  • 5-6 dry red chilies (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tablespoon coriander seeds
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds

    • For the Curry

  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon fennel seeds
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 1 sprig curry leaves
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 5-6 dry red chilies (adjust to taste)
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, mix the prawns with turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, salt, and lemon juice. Allow them to marinate for at least 30 minutes.
  2. In a blender, combine grated coconut, chopped onion, tomatoes, ginger-garlic paste, fennel seeds, poppy seeds, dry red chilies, coriander seeds, and cumin seeds. Blend into a smooth paste by adding a little water.
  3. Heat oil in a pan. Add mustard seeds and fennel seeds. Let them splutter.
  4. Add finely chopped onions and curry leaves. Saute until the onions become golden brown.
  5. Add the masala paste to the pan. Cook the masala on medium heat until the oil separates from it.
  6. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Mix well and cook for a few more minutes.
  7. Add the marinated prawns to the masala and coat them well. Cook for about 5-7 minutes until the prawns are cooked through.
  8. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
