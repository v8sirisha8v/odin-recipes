Nandu Masala

Description

Chettinad Nandu Masala is a crab dish. Known for its bold and spicy flavors, this masala can be eaten with dosa, parotta, aapams, rice, and more!

Ingredients

    For the Crab Masala Paste

  • 1 cup grated coconut
  • 1 tablespoon fennel seeds
  • 1 tablespoon poppy seeds
  • 5-6 dry red chilies (adjust according to spice preference)
  • 1 tablespoon coriander seeds
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1-inch cinnamon stick
  • 4-5 cloves
  • 4-5 black peppercorns
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste

    • For the Crab Masala

  • 500g crab, cleaned and cut into pieces
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 1 large tomato, chopped
  • 1 sprig curry leaves
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon fennel seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions

  1. Dry roast grated coconut, fennel seeds, poppy seeds, dry red chilies, coriander seeds, cumin seeds, cinnamon stick, cloves, and black peppercorns until fragrant. Allow it to cool.
  2. Grind the roasted ingredients along with ginger-garlic paste into a smooth paste using water as needed. Set aside.
  3. Clean the crab thoroughly, removing the outer shell and gills.
  4. Heat oil in a pan. Add mustard seeds and fennel seeds. Let them splutter.
  5. Add chopped onions and curry leaves. Saut√© until the onions become golden brown.
  6. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft and the oil starts separating.
  7. Add the ground masala paste, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Cook the masala paste until the oil separates.
  8. Add the cleaned crab pieces to the masala and coat them well with the masala paste.
  9. Cover the pan and let it cook on low heat for about 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally. The crab will release its juices, and the masala will coat the crab pieces.
  10. Once the crab is cooked and the masala has thickened, garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
